Do you have an NHS.ID?

Use NHS.ID to register with {{ practiceName }}

What is NHS.ID?

NHS.ID is a scheme to fight the growing problem of online identity theft.

It’s secure, and stops someone from pretending to be you.

If you have not used NHS.ID before, it will take you about 10 minutes to get set up. You can access other NHS services securely wherever you see the NHS.ID logo.

If you don’t have an NHS.ID you’ll need to provide:

  • your name and date of birth
  • contact details
  • your address

Other things you’ll be asked

It will help {{ practiceName }} find your medical records if you’re able to provide as much information as you can. This could include:

  • the GP you’re currently registered with if you have one
  • the name or address under which you’re registered with your current GP (if they’re different)
  • details of any allergies, medication and your general health
  • your military service number (if you have one) and enlistment date
  • your NHS number if you know it
How to find your NHS number

An NHS number is a 10 digit number, for example: 943 476 5919

You can find your NHS number:

  • online
  • on a printed prescription
  • on a letter from your GP or a hospital
  • on your NHS medical card if you have one

You can still register without this information.
